IV. REMARKS. place during the period included in this volume. These are the dispute with the Colony of Connecticut for the juris- diction of the Narragansett country, and the difiSculties arising therefrom, and King Philip's war. The corres- pondence and proceedings of the two Colonies, connected with their claims to the Narragansett country, are printed at length, for the first time, in their proper places in this volume. Much of this correspondence was copied a few years since from the records and files in the public Ar- chives of Connecticut, for the Rhode Island Historical Society. From those copies these documents have now been printed. A number of valuable documents have been taken from the manuscript collection of John Carter Brown, Esquire, of Providence, who kindly placed this collection at my dis- posal, for the use of the State. Other documents of value have been obtained from the office of the Secretary of State, of the State of New York. J. R. B. Providence, May, 1857.
